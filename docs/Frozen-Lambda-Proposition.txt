  OBSERVATION 1 — λ is empirically frozen during training
================================================================================
  ⚠ Naming: "Observation" (not "Proposition") — claim is empirical, not derived.
     Per overclaim-discipline ([[feedback-paper-overclaim]]).

  STATEMENT (paper-ready, LOCKED 2026-06-04, REVISED for overclaim discipline)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Let λ = (λ_y, λ_z) ∈ R^{2n_q} denote the quantum encoding scales, trained
  jointly with the variational angles via SPSA + Adam over T episodes.

      ρ_λ := ‖λ_T − λ_0‖_2 / ‖λ_0‖_2  ≪  1                            (Obs 1)

  On the run analysed in this section (T = 1727 episodes, n_q = 12):
  ρ_λ ≈ 0.095. Equivalently, the per-component drift satisfies
  ‖λ_T − λ_0‖_∞ = 0.072 < ε_SPSA = 0.1 — i.e. no individual encoding scale
  moves more than a single SPSA perturbation step over the entire run. We
  refer to this regime as FROZEN-λ. Three tests below characterise its
  empirical signature (T1.1), its analytical justification with a cumulative
  sanity check (T1.3), and motivate the mechanism investigation (T1.2 → Prop 2).

  ⚠ Note: the ε_SPSA comparison MUST use a per-component norm (‖·‖_∞ or RMS),
  not ‖·‖_2 — the latter scales with √n_dim and would yield 0.115 > 0.1, which
  is a misleading dimensional mismatch (ε_SPSA is per-component).

  TEST T1.2 — gradient coherence ratio (in-train diagnostic)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Definition (formal): for each PPO update, SPSA yields per-mini-batch gradient
  vectors ĝ_λ^(b) ∈ R^{2n_q} aggregated over B mini-batches. Define

      r  :=  ‖ḡ_λ‖ / overline(‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖) ,    ḡ_λ := (1/B) Σ_b ĝ_λ^(b)

  Empirical statistics (same CSV as T1.1):

      statistic               min        mean       max
      ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
      mean ‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖          2.39e-01   3.10e-01   4.17e-01
      ‖ḡ_λ‖                   1.13e-02   2.04e-02   3.52e-02
      r                       0.040      0.066      0.100

  r ≪ 1 across the entire run, indicating near-zero batch consistency:
  per-mini-batch gradient estimates have substantial magnitude individually
  but their batch average is suppressed by an order of magnitude.

  TEST T1.3 — Adam update analytical chain + accumulation-regime check
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  With learning rate α = lr_actor_qc = 1e-4 (verified, params.py:235) and
  first-moment decay β_1 = 0.9, the Adam update at steady state satisfies

      Δλ_t = − α · m̂_t / (√v̂_t + ε_Adam)  ∝  ‖ḡ_λ‖  ≈  0.020 .

  ★ ACCUMULATION-REGIME COMPARISON (NOT a quantitative bound or prediction —
  an order-of-magnitude classification of how T·B_PPO ≈ 3·10^4 Adam updates
  aggregate over the run):

      regime                       scaling                   numeric
      ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
      Coherent accumulation        drift ~ α · T·B_PPO        ≈ 3
      Pure random cancellation     drift ~ α · √(T·B_PPO)     ≈ 0.02
      Observed                     ‖λ_T − λ_0‖_2              0.115

  The observed drift falls BETWEEN coherent accumulation and pure random
  cancellation — neither limit applies. This intermediate regime is exactly
  what one expects when per-mini-batch gradients are partially incoherent
  with ratio r ≈ 0.066 (T1.2): the signal does not vanish completely, but
  is suppressed by roughly an order of magnitude relative to the coherent
  ceiling. The smallness of r in T1.2 — not any individual gradient — is
  what governs the accumulation. This regime classification is the empirical
  hook into Proposition 2, which identifies WHY the per-sample gradients are
  incoherent in the first place.

  CLOSING — hook to Proposition 2
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The apparent paradox of vanishing batch-mean despite non-vanishing
  per-sample estimates is the next question. The mechanism is identified in
  Proposition 2.

  PROPOSITION 2 — Gradient directional incoherence (mechanism)
================================================================================
  STATEMENT (paper-ready, LOCKED 2026-06-04, REVISED 2 — math rigor)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The per-mini-batch λ-gradients are healthy in magnitude but point in nearly
  uncorrelated directions, so their average vanishes by DIRECTIONAL INCOHERENCE.
  Formally:

      | 𝔼_{b,b'}[ cos(ĝ_λ^(b), ĝ_λ^(b')) ] |  ≪  1                       (P2.1)

      overline(‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖)  ≫  0                                          (P2.2)

  Expanding the second moment of the batch-mean ḡ_λ = (1/B) Σ_b ĝ_λ^(b):

      𝔼‖ḡ_λ‖²  =  (1/B) · 𝔼‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖²
                  +  ((B−1)/B) · 𝔼⟨ĝ_b, ĝ_{b'}⟩                          (P2.3)

  Empirically (T2.2 below), the pairwise alignment is near zero, which suggests
  that the cross-correlation contribution in (P2.3) is negligible relative to
  the diagonal term, giving

      √(𝔼‖ḡ_λ‖²)  =  O(B⁻¹/²) .                                          (P2.4)

  This makes Adam's 1st moment vanish even though no single gradient is small.
  We call this regime GRADIENT DIRECTIONAL INCOHERENCE.

  Motivated by (P2.4), we expect the observable COHERENCE RATIO

      r  :=  ‖ḡ_λ‖  /  overline(‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖)

  to scale as

      r  ∼  B⁻¹/² ,                                                       (P2.5)

  which is verified empirically in T2.4.

  REMARK — distinction from barren plateau
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  McClean et al. 2018 \cite{mcclean2018barren} prove Var(∂L/∂θ_i) ~ O(2^{−n_q})
  per parameter for sufficiently expressive parameterised circuits — i.e., the
  gradients THEMSELVES vanish. In our setting, T2.1 and T2.6 below show that
  the per-parameter empirical magnitude is substantially above this bound; the
  gradients are alive, only their batch-average vanishes via (P2.4). The
  present observations are inconsistent with a barren-plateau explanation and
  instead support directional incoherence as the dominant mechanism.

  LOGICAL FLOW
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
      T2.1            gradients exist                ‖ĝ_b‖ ≫ 0
        ↓
      T2.2 – T2.3     gradient directions uncorrelated   |𝔼[cos]| ≪ 1
        ↓
      (P2.3)          batch-averaging identity
        ↓
      (P2.4)          mean gradient suppressed       √(𝔼‖ḡ‖²) = O(B⁻¹/²)
        ↓
      (P2.5)          predict coherence ratio        r ∼ B⁻¹/²
        ↓
      T2.4            prediction verified            r_obs ≈ ρ_CLT
        ↓
      ⇒  batch gradient cancellation
      ⇒  Observation 1 explained

  (T2.5 cross-architecture and T2.6 barren-plateau remark are orthogonal
   robustness checks, not part of the main chain.)

  McClean et al. \cite{mcclean2018barren} bound the per-parameter gradient
  variance Var(∂L/∂θ_i) ~ O(2^{−n_q}). For n_q = 12 this gives ≈ 2.4×10⁻⁴.
  Assuming the gradient energy is roughly distributed across the 2n_q = 24
  parameters, the empirical per-parameter mean squared component magnitude is

      𝔼[ ĝ_λ,i² ]  ≈  ‖ĝ_λ^(b)‖² / (2 n_q)  ≈  13.62² / 24  ≈  7.7 ,

  which lies substantially above the McClean bound. Under the additional
  assumption of approximately zero-mean component gradients, this quantity
  coincides with the component variance. The gradients themselves are not
  vanishing; only their batch-averaged direction cancels by (P2.4).

  PROPOSITION 3 — Approximate insensitivity to global rescaling of λ
================================================================================
  STATEMENT (paper-ready, LOCKED 2026-06-04, REVISED 2 — scope honesty)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Define the GLOBAL-RESCALING RESPONSE

      Δ(c)  :=  |𝔼_s[ r_greedy(s; cλ) − r_greedy(s; λ) ]|                  (P3.1)

  where r_greedy(s; λ') denotes the greedy-assignment reward when λ is replaced
  by λ' (with downstream held fixed: aligned phase + equal power), thereby
  isolating λ's effect on the assignment head. Empirically,

      Δ(c)  ≪  1   (relative to reward scale)   for  c ∈ [0.1, 3.0] ,      (P3.2)

  in the regime tested. The greedy-assignment reward is approximately
  insensitive to global rescaling of λ within the multiplicative range tested.

  ⚠ SCOPE — single-direction probe:
  (P3.2) examines only the global-rescaling direction λ → cλ, i.e. one
  one-dimensional subspace of the full encoding-scale space ℝ^{2n_q}. It does
  NOT claim invariance under arbitrary perturbations δλ. A stronger test would
  probe non-rescaling perturbations (e.g. random directions in ℝ^{2n_q}); this
  is left to future work. The conclusions of this section apply along the
  global-rescaling direction only.

  ⚠ Forward-flexibility clause: (P3.2) is asserted only "in the regime tested"
  (D_k=0.10, R_LoS=0.2). Behavior at higher D_k may differ; see refresh-note.

  LOGICAL FLOW
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
      T3.1            global landscape (1D direction)     Δ(c) ≪ 1
        ↓
      T3.2            no obvious state-dependent          G/⟨r⟩ ≪ 1,
                      structure detected                  corr(c*(s), blk) = 0
        ↓
      T3.3            robustness over training            consistent across ckpts
        ↓
      ⇒  reward insensitive along global-rescaling direction in the regime tested
      ⇒  generality across arbitrary δλ ∈ ℝ^{2n_q} remains untested (future work)
        ↓
      hook → Proposition 4

      G  :=  𝔼_s[ max_c r_s(cλ) ]  −  max_c 𝔼_s[ r_s(cλ) ]                 (P3.3)

  richer state-dependent predictor over the full ℝ^{2n_q} space.

  Together, no obvious state-dependent structure was detected that would
  motivate λ(s)-style adaptation along the global-rescaling direction.

  PROPOSITION 4 — Detectable encoder contribution beyond the classical bypass
================================================================================
  Framing summary (compressed, paper-ready)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  We probe the necessity of the encoding stage U_enc(λ, z) NOT by measuring
  reward when U_enc is removed (the system admits a state-independent
  Direct-only fallback that achieves competitive reward, masking the loss), but
  by measuring its contribution to STATE-DISCRIMINATION via per-state policy
  divergences.

  STATEMENT (paper-ready, LOCKED 2026-06-04)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Setup. The assignment head receives h_t = [z_t ‖ o_hat], so the classical AE
  latent z_t provides a state-dependent bypass even when the quantum channel
  o_hat is degenerate. The question is therefore the contribution of U_enc(λ, z)
  BEYOND this bypass.

  Define the U_enc CONTRIBUTION TO POLICY STATE-DISCRIMINATION as

      Δ_KL  :=  𝔼_{s≠s'} [ KL(π(·|s) ‖ π(·|s'))_full
                          − KL(π(·|s) ‖ π(·|s'))_{λ=0} ]                  (P4.1)

  where KL is taken between greedy-assignment per-state policy distributions.
  The motivation is that pairwise KL captures the policy's ability to
  distinguish states; (P4.1) quantifies the change in measured policy-
  discrimination associated with removing the U_enc contribution while
  retaining the classical bypass.

  Empirically,

      Δ_KL  >  0   (relative to bypass-only baseline)                      (P4.2)

  i.e. U_enc-induced state-dependence is detectable in addition to the
  bypass-induced state-dependence. In the regime tested (D_k=0.10, R_LoS=0.2,
  4 checkpoints of result_11, single architecture), the U_enc-attributable
  share is approximately constant.

  ⚠ KL CAVEAT — what "share" means here:
  Throughout this section, "U_enc share" refers SPECIFICALLY to the fraction of
  the measured policy-discrimination signal under the KL metric (P4.1). It does
  NOT denote a share of (i) Shannon information, (ii) general policy
  "capability", or (iii) reward. The KL signal is one (defensible) proxy for
  state-discrimination, and all share statements below are scoped to it.

  LOGICAL FLOW
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
      T4.1            attenuation sweep                  KL(c) monotonic in c
        ↓
      T4.2            bypass-zero contrast               U_enc-alone share ≈ 24%
                                                         of measured KL signal
        ↓
      T4.3            4-ckpt variance band               share ≈ const across ckpts
        ↓
      ⇒  U_enc contributes a measurable, approximately constant share of the
         KL-discrimination signal beyond the classical bypass, in the regime tested
        ↓
      Synthesis with Obs 1 + Prop 2 + Prop 3 (CLOSING below)

  "share" is the direct ratio  KL_alone / KL_baseline. This is a descriptive

  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PROP 4 FINDINGS (paper-ready)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Three findings, in order of strength:

    F1. CONTINUOUS dependence under attenuation. KL(c) is monotonic in c and
        the per-step increments are consistent with a linear trend. The
        absence of a step-change near c=0 is consistent with U_enc contributing
        a continuous functional component of the KL signal, in the regime tested.

    F2. SHARE DECOMPOSITION beyond bypass. With z_t bypass available and λ
        frozen, U_enc-alone accounts for ≈ 24% of the measured KL signal
        (range 23-25% across 4 ckpts of result_11); bypass-alone accounts for
        ≈ 73% (range 70-78%); the two shares sum to ≈ 96%, suggesting limited
        overlap between the two channels.

    F3. SHARE APPROXIMATELY CONSTANT across training. The U_enc-only share
        remains in [22.8%, 25.3%] across the 4 ckpts tested while the absolute
        KL grows ≈ 4.6×. We report this as approximate constancy of the share
        across the 4 ckpts tested, not as a structural property in general.

  CLOSING
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Together with Observation 1, Proposition 2, and Proposition 3, the present
  evidence suggests that, in the regime tested, U_enc(λ, z) with frozen λ
  is a viable architectural choice: the encoding stage contributes a measurable
  share of the policy state-discrimination signal even when its scale parameters
  do not effectively update. Proposition 4 therefore establishes that U_enc(λ, z)
  contributes a measurable and persistent component of policy state-
  discrimination that remains detectable beyond the direct classical bypass,
  even when λ itself is effectively frozen.
